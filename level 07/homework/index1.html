little women


Louisa May Olcott's most famous novel, Little Women, was published in 1868.
The book, the prototypes of the characters of which are members of the author's family, was written in a house in Concord, Massachusetts - Orchad House, which has a rich history of 350 years.
The documentary film "Orchad House: Home of Little Women" about this house was awarded with the Emmy Award in 2019.
